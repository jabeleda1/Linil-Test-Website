Our Policies

Private Policy

How do we typically use or share your information?


Health Information

During treatment, we can use the health information you give us and share it with other professionals who are treating you. This is to ensure you receive the absolute best care we can offer with the information provided. For example, if a doctor is treating you for an injury, they can asks another doctor about your overall health condition and use that information to help improve your health. Your health and safety is important to us, so we use and share your information when it primarily pertains to running our practice, improving your care, and contacting you when necessary.


Billing and Insurance

When using our services, you will be able to provide payment using various insurance plans like Medicare and Medicade. To verify and receive payment, we can use and share the health information you provide us to contact insurance companies under your care. For example, we give information about you to your health insurance plan so it will pay for your services.


Public Health and Safety

To strengthen the awareness about those in need of care, we can share health information about you for certain situations such as preventing disease, helping with product recalls, reporting adverse reactions to medications, reporting suspected abuse, neglect, or domestic violence, and preventing or reducing a serious threat to anyone's health or safety. This also includes sharing your health information with other medical professionals or practices in our field of work for the purposes of health research.


    Complying with the Law

    As stated by the law, we will have to share information about you if the state or federal laws require it. This includes the Department of Health and Human Services if they wants to see that we're complying with federal privacy law. Additionally, in the case of a legal matter, we can share information about you in response to a court or administrative order, or in response to a subpena.


    Work Compensation, Law Enforcement, and Government

    We can use or share health information about you for workers' compensation claims, law enforcement purposes or with a law enforcement official, with health oversight agencies for activities authorized by law, or for special government functions such as military, national security, and presidential protective services.


    Organ and Tissue Donation

    When it comes to donations, we can share health information about you with organ procurement organizations.


    Medical Examiner or Funeral Director

    In the case of an individual or family member passing away, we can share health information with a coroner, medical examiner, or funeral director.
