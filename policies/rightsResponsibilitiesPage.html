Our Policies

Patients Rights and Responsibilities

High Quality Care and Involvement in Your Care

At Linil's Visiting Nurses, Inc. you the right to the following:

  • Receive the highest quality and most cost-effective care available from any healthcare provider of your choice.
  • Receive care from us only if we can provide care at the level you require.
  • Be given information about emergency preparedness, and what to do in case of emergency.
  • Make informed decisions about care and treatment plans.
  • Participate in all aspects of planning your care, and be advised of all potential risks or outcomes you may expect as a result of your care.
  • Refuse any part or all of your care, treatment or services without fear of reprisal or discrimination, to the extent that your refusal or discontinuance of service threatens to compromise your quality of care, which may result in an appropriate transfer to another source of care.
  • Participate or refuse to participate in research, investigational studies or clinical trials, without your access to care, treatment and services being affected.
  • Know the names and professional qualifications of the clinical team members responsible for providing your care.
  • Know what to expect from your clinical team, what kinds of services they will provide, how often these services will be expected, and all potential outcomes, risk, problems and barriers to treatments that may occur.
  • Know about participating in changes to your plan of care.
  • Have your caregivers and family members involved in your medical decision making whatever appropriate or desired, and approved by you or your designated person when allowed by law.
  • Receive notice before your home health services are terminated at least forty-eight hours (48) prior to the termination date.

    • Accessible Communication

    Persons with Limited English Proficiency
    You have the right to receive information in your preferred language to facilitate your care.

  • All foreign language assistance may be provided to you at no additional cost.
  • We are able to provide interpretation services in more than 100 different languages using telephone interpretation and video remote interpretation.
  • If you would like to request interpretation services, please let your case manager or any one of your assigned nurses or therapist know.

    • Persons with Disabilities or Accessibility Needs
    You have the right to receive information through accessible websites, the provision and use of auxiliary aids or services to facilitate your care.

  • We provide special needs services, including for those with impaired hearing-such as American Sign Language (ASL) or sight impairment, to you at no additional cost.
  • If you would like to request accessibility services, please let your case manager or any one of your assigned nurses or therapists know.

    • Financial Rights

    At Linil's Visiting Nurses, Inc. you the right to the following:

  • Be told, verbally and in writing, of our billing policies and payment procedures prior to the start of any services.
  • Be informed of the extent to which payment may be expected from Medicare, Medicaid, or commercial insurance which may cover your services.
  • Be told, verbally and in writing, of any charges that may not be covered by Medicare, Medicaid, or commercial insurance, and what amount of charges you may be expected to be liable for.
  • Receive, within thirty (30) days of your written request, of all bills for services submitted to your insurance company or to you.
  • Be told by the agency, verbally and in writing, within thirty (30) days of becoming aware of any changes in your expected charges or changes to your insurance.

    • Dignity and Ethical Treatment

    At Linil's Visiting Nurses, Inc. you the right to the following:

  • Receive information about the ownership and management of our home health agency.
  • Have a relationship with our staff based on honesty and the highest standards of ethical conduct.
  • Be treated with respect, courtesy and consideration in all aspects, including respect for your unique cultural, psychosocial, spiritual, and personal beliefs or preferences, and have this same right extended to your family members and caregivers.
  • Be treated without regard for race, color, creed, political affiliation, religion, ethnicity, martial status, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, mental or physical disability or veteran status.
  • Receive information about any beneficial relationships with a referral source the agency may have that results in profits for the referring individual or entity.
  • Be free of any mental, physical, financial abuse, neglect or exploitation of any kind.
  • Be free to voice complaints or grievances regarding your care, treatment or services at any time, free from fear of retaliation or reprisal.
  • Create an advance directive, receive information regarding advance directives, and have your healthcare provider comply with your advance directive and address your sihes regarding end of life decisions in accordance with the law.

    • Privacy Rights

    At Linil's Visiting Nurses, Inc. you the right to the following:

  • Personal privacy and security whenever receiving care, treatment or services.
  • Confidentiality of your protected health information
  • Receive information regarding the use and disclosure of your protected health information.
  • Receive information regarding the agency's confidentiality policies and privacy practices and how protected health information is used, disclosed, protected and handled.
  • Refuse to be filmed or photographed for purposes unrelated to your diagnosis and treatment.

    • Patients Medical Records

    At Linil's Visiting Nurses, Inc. you have the right to request a copy of your medical record and any other information we have about you. We will provide you a copy or summary of your health information within 30 days of you request. However, know that we may charge a reasonable cost-based fee.


    Sharing Confidential Information

    In a situation you need to request confidential information, you can ask us to contact you in a specific way (for example, home or office phone) or to send mail to a different address and we will approve all reasonable requests.

    As a patient, you have the right to ask us not to use or share certain health information for treatment, payment, or our operations. However, we are not required to agree to your request, and we may say "no" if it would affect your care. If you pay for a service or health care item out-of-pocket in full, you can ask us not to share that information for the purpose of payment or our operations with your health insurer. We will say "yes" unless a law requires us to share that information.

    You can ask for a list (accounting) of the times we've shared your health information for six years prior to the date you ask, who we shared it with, and why. We will include all the disclosures except for those about treatment, payment, and heatlh care operations, and certain other disclosures (such as any you asked us to make). We'll provide one accounting a year for free but will charge a reasonable, cost-based fee if you ask for another one within 12 months.


    Choose Someone to Act for you

    If you have given someone medical power of attorney or if someone is your legal guardian, that person can exercise your rights and make choices about your health information. We will make sure the person has this authority and can act for you before we take any action.


    Privacy Notice

    You can ask for a paper copy of this notice at any time, even if you have agreed to receive the notice electronically. We will provide you with a paper copy promptly.


    File a Complaints

    As a patient, you have the right to complain if you feel we have violated your rights by contacting us using the information in this website. You can file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights by sending a letter to 200 Independence Avenue, S.W., Washington, D.C. 20201, calling 1-877-696-6775, or visiting www.hhs.gov/ocr/privacy/hippa/complaints/. We will not retaliate against you for filing a complaint.


    Unresolved Compliants

    If you are dissatisfied with the result of the compliant process, you may file a compliant with any of the regulating authorities responsible for overseeing the care we provide you, at any time and without fear of retaliation. You may also contact these agencies at anytime, even if you have not gone through the entire compliant process.
